Abstract

Numerous works have recently integrated 3D camera control into foundational text-to-video models. However, existing methods suffer from three core limitations: low resolution, object instability, and motion inconsistency, which hinder their applications in real-world scenarios. Addressing the low-resolution challenge, our solution introduces a dual-branch fusion framework operating in a latent space, built upon the Diffusion Transformer (DiT) architecture. To mitigate object instability such as shape distortion or object disappearance, we employ a novel semantic guidance strategy that incorporates vision-language features from pre-trained foundational models, ensuring temporal stability in object appearances. For motion inconsistency, we decouple video motion into camera movements and object dynamics: a spatial-temporal encoder with Pl√ºcker embedding models camera trajectories for viewpoint manipulation, while an object-aware motion disentanglement mechanism separates foreground objects via high-frequency decomposition and synthesizes natural dynamics through diffusion, independent of global camera movements. Extensive experiments demonstrate that GimbalGen achieves a notable improvement across three key limitations, achieving superior performance in foreground-background consistency under complex dynamic scenes.

Framework


Framework of GimbalGen The overview of our dual-branch fusion framework which is built upon the DiT architecture. To enhance object stability, we introduce a semantic guidance strategy that leverage vision-language features from a foundational model to enrich the representation of global scenes. For motion consistency, we decouple video motion into camera movements and object dynamics. The Camera Condition Module encodes input camera trajectories, while the Object-Aware Motion Disentanglement mechanism separates object motion from camera motion via extracting an object-aware mask.
